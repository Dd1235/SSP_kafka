## The bursty producer


- real world traffic is never perfectly 10,000 msg/sec. Burst controller

-rate 1000 -burst-rate 20000 -burst-duration 3s -burst-period 12s

For 9 seconds, atomic current rate is 1000,for 3 seconds its 20,000
Producers read current Rate continuously, and recalculate their internal time.Ticker intervwhals.

See how quickly lag spikes during the 3-second burst. 



## Phased Consumer Recovery

If a downstream database goes offline, consumer lag will skyrocket. What if it comes back online. How fast can the consumer group drain the massive backlog?
We need a recovery drain rate.

Phase 1 (the outage) - first 20 seconds, 12 partition goroutines sleep for 10ms per message. Hopelessly slow.

The switch: at T=20 a phaser goroutine wakes up and flips an atomic variable called currentDelayNs from 10ms to 0ms.

The Recovery: Consumers stop sleeping. Suddenly process messages as fast as CPU allows.

By measuring recovery drain rate of the system, if I know that backlog is x, I can measure how much time it will take to recover.
